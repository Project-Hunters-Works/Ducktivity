Mind Mapping

Testing class

Make your communication more efficient and clear.

Mind mapping is a powerfull way of organising your ideas. Main idea of mind mapping is putting a main topic/idea in the center and decomposing it to sub-branches.This helps you to focus on one sub-branch at a time and it is way easier than diving into main idea. The sub-branches should be relative to the central idea.You can create a mind map with a paper, using a board or on your computer.

Where can you use mind mapping?

  • on your assignments
  • writing an essay
  • exam preperation
  • measuring knowledge
  • brainstorming etc.

Creating a Mind Map

Mind maps don't need any structures, it is just up to you to design a mind map. If it improves your understanding that is the result that we want.After creating a mind map you can always add new sub-branches or sub-goals. When using this method, you can map your ideas with the help of symbols. Also it is always better to use lots of colors, since it helps you to memorize easily. If you are using a board you can use stickers to make it even better.

Benefits of Mind Mapping Technique

  • It helps to keep informations in memory more permanently thanks to graphs.
  • Mind mapping constructs bridges between informations. It improves the ability to make connections between ideas or concepts.
  • It helps you to understand the complex topics in a much easier way.
  • It increases productivity and this way you can spend less time understanding topics.

An example of using mind map

You are a student and you need to learn how to do basic math operations like subtraction, addition, multiplication or division. You may follow these steps:

  1. Your main idea is to learn math operations. So take a paper or use your computer and draw a circle in the center of a page and write math operations in it.
  2. You have 4 sub-tasks to complete. Think the circle as a root of a tree. Now draw 4 branches each one represents 1 task. Draw circles to the end of the branches and write a task in it.
  3. Now your main idea is broken down into smaller parts. This way you will just focus on one task and this will make you progress faster.
